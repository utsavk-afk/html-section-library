Founder & Letterpress Printer

Margaux Deveraux

she / her · answers to Mo before noon

I set metal type by hand because the machine that replaced it forgot how to leave a mark you can feel with your thumb.

Margaux runs The Bindery, a two-person letterpress studio that prints wedding suites, poetry chapbooks, and the occasional stubborn business card. She started in 2009 with a borrowed proof press and a drawer of Caslon someone was about to throw in a dumpster. She kept the type. The dumpster went empty.

“Good printing is mostly patience and a little ink — in that order.”

Her work leans quiet: deep impression, generous margins, one confident accent color and nothing it has to compete with. Clients tend to arrive asking for something “elegant” and leave with something specific instead. She teaches a Saturday workshop each spring, mostly so the presses get used by hands other than her own.
