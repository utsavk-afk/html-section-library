01 The Eye's First Landing

Start heavy in the top-left, where every reader's gaze arrives.

Western readers anchor their first fixation in the upper-left quadrant before the page has fully loaded. Put the single most important line there — a name, a promise, a number — and let it earn the scan that follows.

One focal point per screen. Not two competing headlines fighting for the same corner.

02 The Diagonal Drop

Sweep across, then down and back to build a natural Z.

After the top edge, the eye cuts diagonally to the lower-left, then travels right again. Alternate a visual and a text block across each row so the layout traces that Z instead of stacking into a monotonous column.

Vary the rhythm: tighten spacing on quiet rows, open it up where a claim needs air.

03 The Resting Corner

Land the ask in the bottom-right, the last place they look.

The Z closes where attention naturally exits. Reserve that corner for the one action worth taking — a single button, a signature, a call. Crowd it with three choices and the momentum you built dissolves.

End the way a good paragraph ends: deliberately, not by running out of room.
