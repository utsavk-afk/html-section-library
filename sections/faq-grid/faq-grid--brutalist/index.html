Q.01

Is the library actually free?

Yes. Every section is MIT-licensed. Copy the file, paste it into your project, ship it to production. No attribution required, no signup wall, no "free for personal use" trap. Use it for client work and keep the invoice.

MIT · No Catch
Q.02

Do I need a build step?

Never. Each section is a single self-contained index.html — no npm, no Webpack, no Tailwind config. The only external call is Google Fonts.

Q.03

Will it work in Claude Code?

Built for it. Drop a section into your prompt, say "match this style", and the model has a full working reference to extend — tokens, CSS, and markup all in one paste.

Q.04

How do I change the colors?

Edit the :root{} block at the top. Swap --acid and every accent updates at once. All tokens live in one place on purpose.

Q.05

Is it responsive out of the box?

Yep. Every section ships with a @media(max-width:640px) breakpoint tested down to 320px. Nothing overflows horizontally — the two-column grid collapses to one on mobile automatically.

320px → 1440px
Q.06

Can I mix sections from different styles?

You can, but expect visual whiplash. Brutalist next to soft-minimal reads like two sites. Pick one lane per page for a clean result.

Q.07

What about accessibility?

Semantic landmarks, real heading order, and AA contrast on text. The high-contrast brutalist palette helps — black on acid-yellow clears WCAG with room to spare.

Q.08

How often do new sections land?

Weekly-ish. New types get added as they're battle-tested on real builds — no filler drops just to pad the count.
