The House Position

We would rather ship one honest paragraph
than ten persuasive ones nobody believes.

Most content is written to fill a slot. Ours is written to change a mind, or it doesn't run. Five things we won't move on

01
Clarity is not a style choice. It is the whole job.

If a sentence needs a second reading to survive, it fails the first draft. We cut before we decorate — every time, no exceptions.

02
A ranking with no reader behind it is a vanity metric.

Position three means nothing if the click bounces in four seconds. We optimize for the person, and the algorithm follows.

03
We say no to the brief before we say yes to the work.

The best deliverable is sometimes half the pages you asked for. We would rather lose the upsell than pad the sitemap.

04
Opinions age well. Keyword stuffing ages by Thursday.

A point of view is the one thing an AI summary can't flatten. So we take one — on the record, with our name on it.

05
If we wouldn't send it to a friend, we don't publish it.

That's the entire quality gate. It is stricter than any checklist, and it has never once let a filler post through.

None of this is a growth hack. It is just what happens when you refuse to waste a reader's afternoon.
