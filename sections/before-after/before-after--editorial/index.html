The Handoff, Rewritten

Same brief.
A different Tuesday morning.

We took one real onboarding flow and ran it two ways. The work didn't change — the friction did. Here is the before, and here is what replaced it.

01  /  BeforeThe old way

A ticket sits in three inboxes and nobody owns the reply.

  • Six tools, one contextThe account manager pastes the same summary into Slack, the CRM, and a doc.
  • Approvals by memorySomeone has to remember who signs off — usually after it ships.
  • Status is a meetingThe only way to know where things stand is to interrupt three people.
9 days median time from kickoff to first live page

02  /  AfterThe new way

One thread. One owner. The next step is already assigned.

  • Context travels with the workThe summary is written once and every tool reads from it, not copies of it.
  • Approvals are a step, not a huntThe right reviewer is named before the draft exists, and pinged when it's ready.
  • Status answers itselfAnyone can open the thread and see the stage, the owner, and the blocker.
2 days median time from kickoff to first live page
